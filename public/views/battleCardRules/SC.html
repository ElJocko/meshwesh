SCYTHED CHARIOTS AND STAMPEDES

Rules Change

Marked stands of enemy infantry suffer degraded defenses against enemy cavalry in some situations.

Open-order foot fight normally (they suffer no ill effect). Close order foot with an active Supporting Bowmen or Elephant Screen battle card also fight normally. Close-order foot without such support fight as follows for this turn only:

  • They are shattered by Javelin Cavalry or Elite Cavalry.
  • They fight at -1 against Horsebow, Knights, or Cataphracts.

Timing

The choice to use this ability is made during the friendly movement phase after command dice are rolled, before any movement begins.

Place a marker (usually a 2mu x 2mu square stand showing scythed chariots, stampeding cattle, or whatever type of sacrificial attack is appropriate for that army) such that it clearly marks one stand of close-order foot (or two adjacent stands of close-order foot who are in side edge contact) as being the stands “attacked” by the scythed chariots or the livestock stampede.

Remove the marker once the combat phase is complete.

This is an event card and is expended when used; flip the card upside down to mark that it is no longer available for use in the current battle.

It is not appropriate for a player to change their mind about using this card while moving their stands – timing these attacks was a difficult tactical problem. Once it is used, it is used.

Cost

2 points.

In Grand Triumph one battle card may be purchased for each General that has the option of the battle card in the army lists; the battle card will only modify combats in which that General’s troops are involved.
