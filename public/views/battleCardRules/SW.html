STANDARD WAGON

Rules Change

Instead of having a camp, armies with this battle card may deploy a Standard Wagon. Standard here is used in the sense of flag or banner, not in the sense of normal or usual. The Standard Wagon acts as, and replaces, the camp. In many ways it acts exactly as a camp:

  • It may not move once deployed.
  • It fights exactly as a camp.
  • It may be garrisoned like a camp.
  • If defeated in combat it counts as 8pts lost in addition to any garrison, exactly as a camp.

The differences between Standard Wagons and camps are as follows:

  • A Standard Wagon is depicted as a rectangular base 2mu in depth and between 4 and 6 mu wide with a Carroccio or other standard wagon modeled on the base. The long axis should be positioned parallel to the player’s base edge.
  • It is deployed as part of the Central Deployment (i.e., not when camps are normally deployed).
  • It must be deployed in open terrain in the deployment area in the center third of the map (i.e., not on the player’s base edge).
  • Friendly foot stands in side or rear edge contact with the Standard Wagon fight at advantage:
    • +1 in close combat.
    • Ranged combat attacks against them suffer a -1 modifier.
  • Note that friendly troops cannot pass through a camp. This means that the camp will block friendly troops falling back, to their possible detriment.

Timing

The choice to use this ability is made when a camp would normally be deployed.

Cost

None.

This is a free army ability, so long as the other conditions are met.
